# Interview Techniques (English Edition)

Use this reference when planning or running user interviews.

## Core Principles
- Ask one question at a time.
- Prefer open questions over yes/no questions.
- Probe behavior, not opinion only.
- Separate facts from assumptions.
- Capture direct quotes for evidence.

## Recommended Session Structure (30–45 min)
1. Warm-up and consent
2. Context and recent behavior
3. Key moments (friction, workarounds, decisions)
4. Deep probes (why, why now, what changed)
5. Closing and reflection

## Probe Patterns
- **Laddering:** Feature → consequence → value
- **5 Whys:** Symptom → root cause
- **Critical incident:** Best/worst real event
- **Cognitive walkthrough:** Step-by-step reconstruction

## Question Quality Checklist
Good:
- "Walk me through what happened when..."
- "What did you do next?"
- "What made that difficult?"

Avoid:
- Leading questions
- Double-barreled questions
- Hypothetical-only questions

## Output Contract
After interviews, produce:
- Evidence log (quotes + tags)
- Theme candidates
- Assumptions list
- Confidence level (HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW)

## Minimum Quality Bar
- At least 5 relevant participants for qualitative direction
- Consent documented
- Transcript or reliable notes available
- Findings linked to evidence
